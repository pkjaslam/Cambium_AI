Model validation review: AquaCrop v6.1, winter wheat, Kimberly, Idaho.

Verdict: Not ready for operational use in predicting crop yield under water stress. Confidence level: Low.

Reasoning against operational readiness:

1. Water-stress performance is untested. The task asks specifically about prediction under water stress, yet nothing in the materials reports yield across a water-supply gradient or under deficit-irrigation treatments. The 2021 validation is a single unspecified season; there is no evidence it spanned dry-down or stressed conditions. [tier: Open]

2. Validation is a single site-year. One season at one site (2021, Kimberly) cannot characterize year-to-year climate variability. A single point gives no distribution of error, no coverage of wet vs. dry years. [tier: Asserted, standard model-evaluation practice]

3. Validation and calibration share the same site (stated in the material). Same-site validation is closer to an in-sample consistency check than an independent test of transferability. [tier: Proved — the material states validation was at the calibration site]

4. Fit statistics are strong but scope-limited. RMSE = 0.42 Mg ha-1, rRMSE = 8.3%, R2 = 0.91 are good numbers on their face, but they are computed on this one same-site season. [tier: Code-verified for the reported values as stated; the readiness inference from them is Open]

What would be needed before an operational go: independent validation at sites and years not used in calibration; explicit evaluation under a range of water-supply / deficit levels; multi-year testing; uncertainty quantification.
