Assessment

The reported FHB fungicide efficacy is weakly supported, and the citation offered as corroboration does not support the claim. Confidence in the trial's own headline number is moderate; confidence that the cited reference backs it is zero.

1. The citation does not support the efficacy claim (critical failure).
Material 0 attributes to "Paul et al. (2018)" a "mean 52% reduction across 19 states in a large meta-analysis." Material 1 resolves that reference as: Paul PA et al. (2018) Baseline sensitivity and genetic structure of Fusarium graminearum populations. Phytopathology 108:873-882. This is a baseline-sensitivity / population-genetics study, not an efficacy meta-analysis. Therefore the "52% reduction across 19 states" claim is unverifiable as cited and must not be treated as fact — this is a citation-content mismatch.

2. The trial's own number is plausibly reported but not independently verifiable.
The 42% reduction (±8% SE) from a 3-year, 5-location Idaho trial is internally coherent. However, no raw data, replication counts, or statistical test are provided, so the 42% and its SE are Asserted, not reproducible from the materials.

3. Confidence warranted.
- On the efficacy effect itself: moderate.
- On the claim as evidenced by the citation: none. The one external citation is mismatched.

Recommendation: Treat the 42% Idaho result as a preliminary, single-study finding pending release of the raw data. Do not cite Paul et al. (2018) in support of efficacy. Overall confidence: low-to-moderate, capped by the broken citation.
