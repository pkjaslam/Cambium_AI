Correlation was found between soil microbial diversity and drought-related yield loss across 18 fields. Here is what the single observational study supports and what it does not.

## What CAN be concluded

- An association exists in this sample: higher microbial diversity is associated with lower drought-related yield loss (Code-verified from material 0: r=0.52, p=0.03).
- The relationship is moderate in strength and positive in the "diversity up, loss down" direction. r=0.52 implies roughly 27% of variance shared (r-squared about 0.27), so most variance is unexplained (Proved arithmetic from the stated r).
- The association is statistically significant at conventional alpha=0.05 in this dataset (Code-verified: p=0.03), though see caveats below.

## What CANNOT be concluded

- Causation. The study is explicitly observational with no manipulation (Asserted per material 0), so it cannot show that microbial diversity causes resilience. Reverse causation and confounding (soil type, organic matter, moisture retention, management, climate) are unaddressed (Open).
- Magnitude of any causal effect, or a predicted yield benefit from increasing diversity (Open).
- Generalizability beyond these 18 fields. With n=18 the estimate is imprecise (Open).
- Robustness. No confidence interval, adjustment for confounders, or sensitivity analysis is reported (Open).

## Bottom line

The study justifies a hypothesis worth testing, not a conclusion. To move toward causation you would need a manipulative design, replication across sites and seasons, and models that adjust for the obvious soil and management confounders.
