The cover crop plots show a higher mean MBC, a difference of roughly 70 mg C kg⁻¹ (~28%). All three cover crop values exceed all three control values, which is a consistent pattern.

## Why I can't confirm statistical significance with confidence

1. No test was run. As the note states, no statistical test has been performed. While a two-sample t-test on these numbers would likely return a small p-value, that result would rest on assumptions that n=3 cannot support.
2. Very small sample (n=3 per group). With three observations per group, you cannot reliably check normality or equal variance, and the estimate of variability is unstable.
3. Convenience sample, not randomized. The note flags this as a convenience sample. Non-random selection means the difference could reflect pre-existing plot differences rather than the treatment.
4. Single season, no replication over time.

## Conclusion

The pilot data are promising and hypothesis-generating: cover crop plots had consistently higher MBC (~28% greater on average). However, I would not claim a statistically significant increase. At best, one could say the observational difference is suggestive but inconclusive, with low confidence given the tiny, non-randomized convenience sample and the absence of a performed test, baseline data, and real replication.

Recommendation: Treat this as pilot evidence to justify a properly powered, randomized/blocked field trial with baseline MBC measurements and adequate replication.
