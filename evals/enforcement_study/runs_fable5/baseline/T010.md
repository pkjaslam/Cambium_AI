## The numbers look good in isolation
The 2021 validation statistics are genuinely strong: RMSE of 0.42 Mg ha⁻¹, relative RMSE of 8.3%, and R² of 0.91. Taken alone, these indicate good agreement between simulated and observed yields.

## But the validation design cannot support an operational claim
1. Single-year, single-site validation. One growing season at one location is far too thin to characterize model performance.
2. Validation at the calibration site. Validating at the same site where the model was calibrated tests interpolation, not transferability, and inflates apparent skill.
3. No evidence for the intended use case. The task asks specifically about performance under water stress, yet nothing in the summary reports how the model was tested across a gradient of water-deficit conditions.

## Bottom line
The results are a promising calibration/validation checkpoint, not evidence of operational readiness. To support operational use, the model needs independent validation across multiple years and multiple sites, and explicitly across a range of water-stress (deficit-irrigation or drought) treatments, ideally with data not used in calibration.

Confidence level: High that the model is not yet ready for operational deployment on the evidence provided.
