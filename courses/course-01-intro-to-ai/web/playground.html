AI playground

Three hands-on labs that make Module 2 click. Everything runs right here in your browser. Nothing is sent anywhere. Exploring all three is part of the course: the quiz unlocks only after the lecture, the flashcards, and these labs.

1 · See text become tokens

Models never see words. Type anything and watch it get chopped into token-like chunks, each of which becomes just a number.

Words: 0  ·  Estimated tokens: 0  ·  a handy rule: tokens ≈ words × 4/3
This is a simplified splitter for intuition. Real tokenizers (like the ones inside GPT or Claude) learn their chunks from data, but the idea is exactly this.

2 · Be the language model

This tiny model read a few paragraphs and counts which word tends to follow which. Type a phrase and see its next-word bets, exactly the game ChatGPT plays with trillions of words.

Its continuation will appear here. Low temperature picks the safest word every time; high temperature gambles.
Trained on ~150 sentences in your browser. Frontier models play this same game with hundreds of billions of dials instead of a counting table, but the core trick is identical: predict, append, repeat.

3 · Train a model by hand, then let training do it

The green dots are data. Your curve is a three-dial model. Drag the sliders to reduce the error, then press auto-train to watch gradient descent nudge the dials the way real training nudges billions of weights.

Error (loss): -
Training a real model is this exact loop, guess, measure error, nudge every dial downhill, repeated trillions of times on tens of thousands of chips. Now you have felt it.
