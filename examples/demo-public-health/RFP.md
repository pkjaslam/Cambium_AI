## Background
Wastewater-based epidemiology (WBE) has demonstrated value as an early-warning signal for
community-level infectious disease burden — reaching populations who may never seek clinical care.
Recent experience with pathogen surveillance in municipal wastewater has created momentum for
systematic, equity-centred expansion to under-resourced communities that rely on smaller
decentralised sewage systems (package plants, septic clusters). This call seeks proposals to
design, pilot, and evaluate a WBE early-warning programme tailored to such communities.

## Research Priorities
1. Site selection rationale that centres equity (rural, low-income, or otherwise underserved areas).
2. A validated laboratory method for pathogen detection and quantification in low-flow systems.
3. A community-facing reporting mechanism that is actionable without requiring clinical infrastructure.
4. An evaluation framework measuring both epidemiological sensitivity and community trust.
