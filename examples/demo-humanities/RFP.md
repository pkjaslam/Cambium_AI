## Background
Hundreds of thousands of pages of 19th-century English-language newspapers have been digitised by
national libraries and made available as raw OCR text. Historians and literary scholars have used
individual runs intensively, but large-scale, cross-title corpus analysis remains rare. This call
invites proposals to apply computational text-analysis methods to these corpora in order to surface
patterns in language, ideology, and representation that are invisible to close reading alone.

## Research Priorities
1. Demonstrate a humanities research question that motivates the corpus study.
2. Apply at least one reproducible computational method (e.g. topic modelling, word-embedding
   shift, named-entity frequency analysis).
3. Interpret findings through a historically informed close-reading of representative passages.
4. Publish a cleaned, documented data subset and the analysis code under an open licence.

## Evaluation Criteria
