# 01 RFP Brief — Voices of the Press: 19th-Century Newspaper Corpus Study (FICTIONAL DEMO)
*Agent: RFP-Analyst | Date: demo | Input: RFP.md*

## Sponsor & ceiling
Fictional Humanities Research Council (HRC) · $95 K / 24 months ·
evaluation: scholarly significance 35 % / methodological rigour 25 % / integration 25 % / open data 15 %.

## Must-haves (disqualifying if missed)
- A clearly articulated humanities research question driving the corpus work.
- At least one reproducible computational method applied to the newspaper texts.
- Close-reading interpretation alongside the computational results.
- Open-licence release of a cleaned data subset and all analysis code.

## Fit assessment
| Criterion | Our capacity | Gap |
|---|---|---|
| Research question (history / literary studies) | Strong — History faculty available | Need lead historian on team |
| Computational methods (NLP / topic modelling) | Strong — Digital Humanities Lab has corpus tools | Minor: OCR quality pre-processing needed |
| Mixed-method integration | Medium — interdisciplinary collaboration required | Action: recruit historian co-PI |
| Open data & code | Standard practice in our lab | Need IP/licence check on digitised source material |

**Overall fit: 8 / 10.**

## Open questions for G1 (Director decides)
1. Which newspaper corpus is in scope — national titles, regional press, or a thematic subset (e.g. abolitionist papers)?
2. Is the digitised source material free of redistribution restrictions (check HathiTrust / BNA terms)?
3. Do we have (or can we partner with) a historian whose questions will anchor the study?

## Recommendation
Pursue, contingent on: (a) confirming a historian co-PI, and (b) verifying redistribution rights for
the planned corpus. The interdisciplinary framing is a strength for this funder; leading with
computational method alone risks a low "integration" score.

**G1 decision needed: pursue / do not pursue / pursue contingent on co-PI and rights confirmation.**
