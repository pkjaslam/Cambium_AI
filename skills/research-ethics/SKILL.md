# Research ethics — responsible conduct at every gate

Ethics isn't a final checkbox; it gates each phase. This skill surfaces obligations early and **blocks
or conditions** work that isn't cleared — but the human makes the call and signs off.

## Screen by area (raise what applies)
| Area | Trigger | What to check |
|---|---|---|
| Human subjects | people, surveys, identifiable data | IRB review/exemption, informed consent, minimal risk, vulnerable populations |
| Animal subjects | vertebrate animals | IACUC protocol, 3Rs (replace/reduce/refine) |
| Privacy & data | personal/sensitive data | FERPA (education), health-data rules, de-identification, secure storage |
| Data sovereignty | Indigenous/community data | CARE principles, community consent & governance |
| Conflict of interest | financial/personal stakes | disclosure, management plan, recusal |
| Authorship & credit | who's an author | contribution criteria (ICMJE-style), order agreed, acknowledgments, no ghost/guest authors |
| Dual use / biosecurity | could enable harm | dual-use review; withhold operational hazard detail |
| AI use | AI-assisted work | disclose AI's role per venue/funder policy; human accountability |

## Decision output
For each gate, return one of: **GO** (no concerns), **CONDITIONS** (proceed after specific actions —
e.g., "obtain IRB exemption", "add COI disclosure"), or **STOP** (do not proceed until resolved). List
the obligation, what triggers it, and the concrete next step.

## Consent & data governance basics
- Consent: purpose, what's collected, risks, voluntariness, withdrawal, contact. Match the IRB protocol.
- Data: collect the minimum, store securely, define retention/sharing, de-identify before analysis where
  possible.

## Guardrails
- This skill **advises**; it does not grant approvals — IRB/IACUC/legal and the PI do that.
- When unsure whether something needs review, flag it for human review rather than assume it's exempt.
- Never help with genuinely dangerous dual-use specifics; raise the concern and stop.
- Record the determination and the human sign-off in the governance ledger.
